TigerTransit/Bus Services
Princeton Universityâ€™s TigerTransit transportation system operates on a fixed route schedule, providing safe, convenient and reliable transportation throughout the Princeton campus and surrounding community.

The TigerTransit transportation system offers stops at academic and administrative buildings; faculty, staff, graduate and undergraduate student housing; Nassau Street; and the Forrestal Campus and Princeton Plasma Physics Lab.

To support the Sustainability and Campus Plans, the TigerTransit fleet is low-floor, fully accessible, run on B20 biodiesel fuel and have bike racks. For more information on the new TigerTransit fleet, click here.

TigerTransit buses are equipped with web-based Global Positioning Systems (GPS) that can track buses in real-time (TigerTracker). Riders will be able to track the location of any TigerTransit bus on any route online or by cell phone.

Other TigerTransit services include stops with shelters, connections to NJ Transit and Princeton Borough FreeB bus, service to local shopping centers, an on-demand service and extended hours. Multi-line TigerTransit stops provide transfers from one line to another.

For service disruptions please contact First Transit directly at 609.587.1070.

The TigerTransit Route Map is also available.
